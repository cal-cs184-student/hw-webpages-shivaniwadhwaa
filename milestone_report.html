Campanile — Milestone Report

Shivani Wadhwa, Roopa Srinivas, Akshat Kishore Shrivastava, Kushagra Srivastava

Summary

We are building a real-time, browser-based renderer of UC Berkeley's Campanile, driven by physically accurate lighting. Every visual parameter — sky color, shadow direction, surface appearance — comes from physical models rather than hand-tuned values.

The scene features a time-of-day slider, a date slider for seasonal variation, weather modes (clear and rain), and bell chimes at the top of each hour. The demo runs entirely in the browser with no installation.

Milestone deliverable achieved: A full 24-hour day cycle with physically correct sun movement, Preetham sky color, and dynamic shadow mapping — running live in the browser.

Accomplishments

Project scaffold Three.js + WebGL 2.0 with orbit camera, HTML sliders, and GitHub Pages deployment.
Solar positioning Solar declination, hour angle, azimuth, and altitude equations anchored to Berkeley GPS (37.87°N, 122.26°W).
Preetham sky shader GPU sky dome with physically correct chromaticity from zenith to horizon, parameterized by sun elevation and turbidity.
Campanile geometry Textured 3D mesh via Meshroom photogrammetry, optimized for 60fps on consumer hardware.
Shadow mapping Directional shadow map driven by the solar position — updates live as the time slider moves.
Interactive UI Time-of-day and date sliders connected to the full lighting pipeline with real-time feedback.

Preliminary Results

Validated

Solar azimuth/altitude matches NOAA tables within <0.5° across all months for Berkeley coordinates.
Preetham sky chromaticity matches reference images from Preetham et al. (1999) at T=2.5.
Day cycle runs at 60fps on M2 MacBook Pro with no visible frame drops during shadow updates.
Campanile mesh renders with correct UV-mapped stone textures at multiple zoom distances.

Issues to address

Preetham shows banding near the sun disk at very low elevations — Hosek-Wilkie may be needed for twilight.
Shadow map resolution needs tuning for distant views; stairstepping visible on ground plane at zoom-out.
GPU utilization is ~35%, leaving headroom for Week 3 rain particles and volumetric fog.
